# LinkedIn Channel Formatter

## Role
Generate a LinkedIn article post — professional, authoritative, data-driven.

## Input
- story.json (verified)
- evidence score
- key data points

## Output
- Article text (≤ 3,000 characters)
- Hero image spec
- Call-to-action

## Structure

### First Paragraph (Hook — 2-3 lines)
"The latest data on [topic] reveals [key finding]. Here's what it means for [affected group]."

### Context (2-3 paragraphs)
- What the story investigates
- Why it matters to professionals in this space
- What changed or what was uncovered

### Key Findings (3-5 bullet points or short paragraphs)
- One data point per paragraph
- Bold the number or statistic
- Connect each finding to its implication

### Analysis (2-3 paragraphs)
- What these findings mean for the sector
- How they compare with historical trends
- What policymakers or businesses should note

### Evidence & Sources
"Evidence Score: {score}% — verified by The Breakdown's editorial team against [N] sources including [top 2 sources]."

### Call-to-Action
"Read the full investigation with interactive charts and maps → [link]"

---

## Formatting
- **Professional tone** — authoritative but accessible, Grade 10 level
- **Line breaks** between paragraphs for readability
- **Bold** key numbers and findings
- **No emojis** (LinkedIn professional convention)
- **No hashtags** (reduces reach on LinkedIn)
- **Hero image**: 1200 × 627 px (LinkedIn share ratio)
- **Character count**: aim for 1,500-2,500, hard cap 3,000

## Rules
1. **First 3 lines must hook** — they show in the feed without "see more".
2. **Every claim must cite** the evidence score and source.
3. **No editorializing** — "essential reading" not "shocking revelation".
4. **Tag relevant organizations** if applicable (@TheBreakdown for brand).
5. **Post time**: 8:00 AM IST on weekdays (professional reading time).
