### Example 1: Evidence Block (Good vs Bad)

**Bad — Claim separated from source:**
> The government's MGNREGA reforms have weakened rural employment guarantees. Critics say the new eligibility criteria will exclude millions of workers. The changes also reduce the number of guaranteed days from 100 to 90. Rural activists have protested across multiple states. According to the Ministry of Rural Development's 2025-26 annual report, person-days of employment dropped by 12% in the first quarter after the reform.

**Good — Evidence immediately follows claim:**
> The government's MGNREGA reforms have weakened rural employment guarantees. According to the Ministry of Rural Development's 2025-26 annual report, person-days of employment dropped by 12% in the first quarter after the new eligibility criteria took effect.
>
> The change reduces guaranteed work days from 100 to 90. This matters because households that depend on MGNREGA for survival — nearly 15 crore families — now face a 10% reduction in their safety net.

### Example 2: Paragraph Length (Bad vs Good)

**Bad — Wall of text (180 words, 8 sentences):**
> The Union Budget 2026 allocated ₹1,20,000 crore to MGNREGA, which represents a 15% increase over the previous year's allocation of ₹1,04,000 crore. However, this increase is significantly lower than the 35% rise in demand for work under the scheme, which has reached record levels in the current fiscal year. The Ministry of Rural Development reported that MGNREGA provided 389 crore person-days of employment in 2025-26, the highest since the scheme's inception in 2006. This surge in demand is being driven by several factors, including rural distress caused by inflation, reduced agricultural incomes, and the lingering effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on informal employment. The gap between allocation and demand means that per-person spending has actually declined in real terms after adjusting for inflation. Opposition parties have criticised the government for not adequately funding the scheme, while activists argue that the budget increase is insufficient to meet the constitutional guarantee of employment. The government has defended its allocation, pointing to the 15% nominal increase and arguing that states must also contribute their share. Economists say the funding gap could lead to delays in wage payments and reduce the scheme's effectiveness as a rural safety net.

**Good — Broken into evidence blocks (avg 60 words, 2-3 sentences each):**
> The Union Budget 2026 allocated ₹1,20,000 crore to MGNREGA. This is a 15% increase over last year's ₹1,04,000 crore [Budget Documents, 2026].
>
> But demand is growing much faster. MGNREGA provided 389 crore person-days of employment in 2025-26 — the highest since the scheme began [Ministry of Rural Development Annual Report, 2026]. That's a 35% jump from the previous year.
>
> The gap between funding and demand means per-person spending has actually fallen. After adjusting for inflation, the real allocation per worker declined by 8%.
>
> The result? Economists warn of delayed wage payments. When funding falls short, states take longer to process payments, and workers bear the cost.

### Example 3: Section Structure

**Question:** Why is MGNREGA demand at a record high?

**Answer + Evidence + Explanation:**
> MGNREGA demand is surging because rural distress is outpacing economic recovery.
>
> The scheme provided 389 crore person-days of employment in 2025-26 — a record [MOSPI, 2026]. This is 35% higher than pre-pandemic levels.
>
> Three factors explain the surge. First, agricultural incomes have not kept pace with inflation. Second, informal sector jobs have not fully recovered after COVID-19. Third, women's participation has risen sharply — they now make up 55% of all MGNREGA workers.
>
> But here's the challenge. The budget hasn't kept up with demand.

**Transition:** This funding gap has direct consequences for the 15 crore families who depend on MGNREGA for survival.

### Example 4: Humanized Transitions

**Instead of:**
> Furthermore, the budget allocation for MGNREGA has not kept pace with inflation. Moreover, the number of households demanding work has increased significantly. Additionally, state governments have been slow to release their matching contributions.

**Write:**
> The budget allocation hasn't kept pace with inflation. But there's another problem. The number of households demanding work has surged — and state governments have been slow to release their share.
>
> The result? Delayed payments for workers who can't afford to wait.

### Example 5: Self-Review Checklist Applied

**Before review:**
> It is important to note that MGNREGA was originally designed as a safety net for rural households. Moreover, the scheme has undergone several changes over the years. Additionally, the current reforms mark a significant shift in its approach. Furthermore, stakeholders have raised concerns about the pace of implementation.

**After review (banned phrases removed, evidence added, sentences shortened):**
> MGNREGA was designed as a safety net for rural households. Since 2006, the scheme has undergone several changes. The current reforms mark a significant shift.
>
> How? The government reduced guaranteed work days from 100 to 90 [MGNREGA Amendment 2025]. Stakeholders — from worker unions to state governments — have raised concerns about what this means for implementation.
