### STORY DNA

**What happened?** US-Israeli military strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities triggered Iranian closure of the Strait of Hormuz, causing a global energy crisis.

**Why did it happen?** Collapse of diplomatic channels, perceived nuclear threat from Iran, US maximalist strategy, Israeli security imperatives, Iranian leadership change enabling hardline response.

**What alternatives existed?** Renewed JCPOA negotiations, limited strikes instead of broad campaign, multilateral diplomatic pressure, covert action instead of overt military.

**Who benefits?** US defense contractors, Israeli security establishment, alternative energy suppliers (US LNG, African producers), shipping companies (higher rates), non-Hormuz oil exporters.

**Who loses?** Indian economy (energy import costs), European consumers, emerging markets, global shipping, Iranian civilians, global climate (energy transition disruption).

**What evidence exists?** Congressional reports, satellite imagery, IEA data, shipping tracking, oil futures data, IAEA inspection records, UN documents.

**What evidence is missing?** Independent nuclear facility damage assessments, accurate Iranian casualty figures, full scope of naval mine deployment, actual Iranian nuclear enrichment status.

**What historians debate?** Was US military action proportional? Could diplomacy have succeeded? Did Iran intend to build a weapon? Was Israeli influence excessive? Long-term strategic consequences for US credibility.

**What policymakers debate?** Future of Hormuz security, diversification timelines, strategic reserve adequacy, alliance reliability, escalation management.

**What economists debate?** Permanent inflation premium from chokepoint risk, energy transition acceleration vs. fossil fuel reinvestment, cost of supply diversification.

**What remains unresolved?** Iran's nuclear status, Hormuz security framework, compensation for affected nations, new deterrence model for chokepoint warfare, impact on global energy transition investment.
