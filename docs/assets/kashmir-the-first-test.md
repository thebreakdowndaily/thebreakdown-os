## Key Research Priorities

1. **V.P. Menon portrait** — The most important missing asset. Menon is the central actor in the accession drama (he flew to Srinagar with the Instrument) but is historically under-photographed. NMML oral history interview may include photographs. Menon's own memoir *The Transfer of Power* (1957) contains limited personal images. If no portrait exists, mark as `impossible` and use document substitute.

2. **Indian Army airlift photographs** — The 27 October 1947 airlift is the most visually dramatic moment of the conflict. IWM IND series should contain official Indian Army photographs. Photo Division (MoD, GoI) is the primary source.

3. **UNCIP meeting photographs** — UN Photo Library (media.un.org) is the canonical source. These should be free to obtain for editorial use.

4. **Tribal column photographs** — LIFE magazine's Kashmir/NWFP coverage is the most likely source of high-quality editorial photographs of the Pashtun fighters. Getty RM license will be required unless PD alternatives are found in IWM or NAI.

5. **Document facsimiles** — The Nehru–Mountbatten correspondence (B-03) is the most important documentary gap. NAI PMO files and the Mountbatten Papers at Southampton University are the dual sources.
