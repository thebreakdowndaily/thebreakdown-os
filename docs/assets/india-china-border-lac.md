## Notes

1. **Disputed boundaries:** All maps and charts depicting the India-China border must use dashed lines for disputed segments per Book of Record #0003. No map may present disputed borders as settled fact.

2. **Tibet status:** The status of Tibet (independent buffer vs Chinese autonomous region) is itself contested. Maps depicting Tibet's geopolitical transformation (C-07) must present both the Indian/academic and Chinese government positions.

3. **Terminology:** Use "Line of Actual Control (LAC)" consistently. India uses this term; China uses "actual line of control" or "traditional boundary." The chapter must note the terminological disagreement where relevant.

4. **Chinese archives:** Primary Chinese archival sources (Central Party History Research Office, PLA Archives, Chinese Academy of Social Sciences) are generally not accessible to foreign researchers. This register relies on published Chinese official documents (MFA position papers, *Chronicle of the Chinese-Indian Border*) and Western/Indian academic literature. Where Chinese sources are cited, their limitations must be noted.

5. **Henderson Brooks Report:** The Henderson Brooks Report (official Indian inquiry into the 1962 war) was declassified in 2021 (partial extract). The full report remains classified. Use the declassified extract for C-04 and the chart data.

6. **Evidence Level definitions:**
   - A: Original object personally verified from the holding archive
   - B: Verified by multiple authoritative sources but object not directly inspected
   - C: Verified via reputable secondary sources only
   - D: Probable but incomplete
   - E: Unverified
