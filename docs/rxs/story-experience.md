# Story Experience

## Purpose

The Story Experience transforms a chapter from a collection of claims into a cognitive journey. It exists to make complex knowledge navigable, verifiable, and memorable — ensuring a reader progresses from not knowing to being able to explain, regardless of their starting depth.

## Reader Journey

**Curiosity.** The reader arrives with a question. It may be precise ("What caused the 1962 war?") or vague ("Tell me about India's early foreign policy"). The journey does not start at the chapter — it starts with the need to know.

**Orientation.** The reader learns what this chapter covers, why it matters, how long it will take, and what they will understand by the end. Learning objectives are visible. The Six Questions are stated. The reader decides whether to commit.

**Context.** Before narrative begins, the reader understands where this story sits in time and space. Historical context, prerequisite knowledge, and connections to earlier chapters are established. The reader is never dropped into the middle.

**Narrative.** The evidence spine: what happened, why it happened, what alternatives existed. This is the primary surface. It is linear because causality is linear — but every point in the narrative offers depth on demand.

**Evidence.** At every claim, the reader can verify. The evidence behind each assertion is reachable in one action. The reader never has to choose between narrative flow and trust.

**Debate.** Where historians disagree, the chapter surfaces the disagreement at the point of the claim. The reader sees who disagrees, on what basis, and what the evidence supports. The chapter does not hide uncertainty.

**Understanding.** The reader can now answer the Six Questions for this chapter. Learning objectives are met. The key takeaways are summarised. The reader could explain this topic to someone else.

**Exploration.** The reader follows connections: related chapters, linked entities, primary sources, counterarguments in other volumes. The chapter is a node in a graph, not a dead end.

**Reflection.** The chapter closes with consequences, meaning, and what remains contested. The reader understands why this knowledge matters today and what questions are still open.

## Reader Questions

Each stage answers one primary question:

- Curiosity — What do I need to know?
- Orientation — What is this about and why should I care?
- Context — What happened before this?
- Narrative — What happened and why?
- Evidence — How do we know this?
- Debate — Who disagrees and on what basis?
- Understanding — What did I learn and why does it matter?
- Exploration — What should I read next?
- Reflection — What is still contested?

## Reader Emotional Journey

Understanding is not only cognitive. Every stage of the Reader Journey carries an emotional counterpart that the Story Experience must intentionally guide.

| Stage | Reader feels |
|-------|--------------|
| Curiosity | "I want to understand." |
| Orientation | "I know where I am." |
| Context | "Now this makes sense." |
| Narrative | "I understand what happened." |
| Evidence | "I believe this." |
| Debate | "It's more complicated than I thought." |
| Understanding | "Now I can explain it." |
| Exploration | "I want to learn more." |
| Reflection | "This changed how I think." |

The purpose of the Story Experience is not to persuade. It is to replace uncertainty with informed understanding while preserving intellectual humility. A reader who finishes a chapter should feel more capable, not more convinced.

Secondary questions at each stage:

- Orientation — How long will this take? What will I learn? Is this source trustworthy?
- Narrative — What alternatives existed? Why did India choose this path?
- Evidence — What is the primary source? What is the confidence level?
- Debate — Which historians hold which positions? What evidence supports each side?
- Exploration — How does this connect to what I already know? Where can I verify this claim independently?

## Reading Modes

**Explorer**

- Purpose: Understand the topic without getting lost in detail.
- Expected reading time: 25–35 minutes for a standard chapter.
- Content depth: Executive summary, narrative spine, key takeaways, learning section.
- Knowledge goal: Can explain what happened and why it matters.

**Scholar**

- Purpose: Engage critically with the evidence and interpretations.
- Expected reading time: 60–90 minutes for a standard chapter.
- Content depth: Full narrative, inline claims with expandable evidence, counterarguments, source cards, thinker profiles.
- Knowledge goal: Can evaluate the evidence behind each major claim and articulate scholarly disagreements.

**Researcher**

- Purpose: Extract verified claims with full source trails for independent use.
- Expected reading time: 2–4 hours for a standard chapter.
- Content depth: Full narrative, all evidence panels expanded by default, primary documents, archival provenance, confidence tiers, related claims across the library.
- Knowledge goal: Can cite a verified claim with its complete source trail from archive to publication.

## Story Anatomy

**Hero.** A single compelling image that establishes the topic's human or historical significance. Pedagogical purpose only — it must teach something about the topic, not decorate the page.

**Executive Summary.** Three to five paragraphs that answer the Six Questions at surface level. A reader who reads only this section should understand what happened and why it matters.

**Key Questions.** The Six Questions stated explicitly. The reader knows what answers the chapter will provide.

**Historical Context.** The background a reader needs before the narrative begins. Prerequisite knowledge, connections to earlier chapters, and the state of the world at the chapter's starting point.

**Narrative.** The evidence spine in chronological or causal order. This is the longest section. It is structured by sections, each addressing a phase of the story.

**Claims.** Every factual assertion in the narrative is a registered claim with a confidence score, evidence link, and optional counterargument. Claims are rendered at the point of assertion — never collected in an appendix.

**Evidence.** The supporting material behind each claim. Evidence summaries are one action from the claim. Source details are one action from the evidence summary. Primary documents are one action from the source.

**Thinkers.** Profiles of key individuals that the reader encounters in the narrative. Each thinker card includes their role, their position on the chapter's central questions, and links to their appearances in other chapters.

**Documents.** Primary sources referenced in the chapter. Each document entry includes provenance, a brief excerpt, and a link to the full text or facsimile.

**Timeline.** A chronological view of the chapter's key events. Events link to the section of narrative that discusses them and to the canonical timeline object.

**Maps.** Spatial context for the narrative. Every geographic reference of significance is accompanied by a map showing relevant boundaries, routes, or distributions.

**Historiography.** How historians have interpreted this topic over time. Shifts in scholarly consensus, major works, and current debates. This section acknowledges that the chapter itself is an interpretation.

**Counterarguments.** Alternative interpretations presented at the point of the claims they challenge. No separate counterargument section — disagreement lives alongside the claim it contests.

**Learning.** Learning objectives restated with outcomes. Key takeaways per major section. Review questions. Glossary of terms introduced in the chapter.

**Further Reading.** Curated list of sources for deeper exploration. Each entry includes a one-sentence explanation of what the reader will learn and why this source is recommended.

**Sources.** Complete list of every source cited in the chapter, formatted for reference. Links to the canonical source objects in the registry.

**Feedback.** An invitation to report errors, suggest improvements, or challenge interpretations. Linked to the specific claim or section the reader is commenting on.

## Cognitive Load

Information complexity increases in layers. The reader controls the depth.

**Never appear before the narrative:** confidence tiers, archival provenance, raw historiography, detailed counterarguments, document facsimiles, source metadata. These assume knowledge of what the chapter is about.

**Always appear before evidence:** the claim itself, inline attribution, learning objectives, key questions. The reader must know what is being claimed before they can evaluate the evidence for it.

**Belongs only in Researcher mode:** full archival provenance, IIIF document viewers, source comparison tools, citation export, confidence score methodology. These serve independent verification, not comprehension.

**Progression rule:** Each layer of detail must be a deliberate action from the previous. The reader is never surprised by complexity. The default state is the most accessible — the explorer path. Every additional layer is chosen.

## Trust Moments

**Confidence badge on every claim.** The reader sees immediately how reliable this assertion is. Verified claims (Tier 1) carry different weight than probable claims (Tier 3). Trust comes from knowing what you are reading.

**Primary document link.** The reader can go directly to the source. Not a citation. Not a summary. The thing itself. This is the most powerful trust signal in the system.

**Counterargument at the point of claim.** The reader sees that the institution does not hide disagreement. Presenting opposing views transparently signals confidence, not weakness.

**Source transparency on every evidence item.** Full provenance: author, publication, date, archive location, verification status. The reader can assess source quality independently.

**Version number and last verified date on every chapter.** The reader knows this content is current. A chapter last verified in 2024 is treated differently than one verified today.

**Change log accessible from any chapter.** The reader can see what has changed, when, and why. A history of transparent corrections builds more trust than a claim of never making mistakes.

**"Balance of Evidence" framing.** When the evidence clearly favours one interpretation, the chapter says so explicitly. When the evidence is mixed, the chapter says that too. Certainty is never overstated.

**Disputed status on contested claims.** The reader is told when historians disagree. The confidence score reflects the dispute. The chapter does not pretend certainty where none exists.

**Inline attribution for every factual assertion.** The reader always knows who said it, where, and in what context. No orphan claims.

**Editorial judgment transparency.** The chapter distinguishes between established fact ("The partition of India occurred on 14–15 August 1947"), historical interpretation ("Nehru's policy of non-alignment was driven primarily by..."), and editorial synthesis ("The most coherent explanation for India's response is..."). The reader always knows which layer they are reading.

## Exit States

**Explorer.** Leaves with a clear understanding of what happened, why it matters, and how it connects to the broader story. Can explain the topic to someone else. Knows where to go next. Has encountered at least one claim with evidence.

**Scholar.** Leaves having critically evaluated the evidence behind major claims. Understands where historians disagree and why. Has consulted at least one primary source or counterargument. Can articulate both the dominant interpretation and the challenges to it.

**Researcher.** Leaves with verified claims and complete source trails. Has independently verified at least one claim against the primary document. Can cite sources with full provenance. Has identified connections to other chapters or entities for further investigation.

## Failure Modes

**1. Reader lost — no orientation.** The chapter drops into narrative without explaining what it is about, why it matters, or what the reader will learn. The reader does not know whether to commit.

**2. Reader overwhelmed — jargon without definition.** Specialised terms appear without explanation. The reader encounters "non-alignment," "Panchsheel," or "historiography" without inline definition. Comprehension breaks.

**3. Reader distrusts — evidence hidden.** Claims are asserted without visible attribution. The reader cannot tell whether a statement is established fact or editorial opinion. Trust erodes.

**4. Reader stranded — no timeline.** Events are discussed without chronological context. The reader cannot place the narrative in time or understand causal sequence.

**5. Reader misled — conclusions before context.** The chapter states its conclusions before establishing the evidence that supports them. The reader is asked to accept before understanding.

**6. Reader abandoned — no next step.** The chapter ends without offering continuation. The reader finishes and has no idea what to read next. The journey stops.

**7. Reader misinformed — false certainty.** The chapter presents contested interpretations as settled fact. Confidence scores are missing or misleading. The reader leaves with unwarranted certainty.

**8. Reader bored — no depth on demand.** The chapter provides only surface-level narrative. The reader who wants to verify or explore deeper has no path to do so.

**9. Reader frustrated — no progress indicator.** The reader cannot tell how much of the chapter remains, how long it will take, or where they are within the structure. Long chapters feel endless.

**10. Reader ignored — feedback disappears.** The reader reports an error and hears nothing. No acknowledgement, no follow-up, no visible change. The reader learns that feedback does not matter.

## Immutable Rules

**1. Narrative before evidence, evidence before debate.** The reader must understand what is claimed before they can evaluate the evidence for it. They must see the evidence before they can evaluate competing interpretations. This ordering is never reversed.

**2. Every claim carries a visible confidence indicator.** No claim appears without signalling its evidentiary status. The reader always knows whether they are reading established fact, supported interpretation, or editorial synthesis.

**3. Evidence must be reachable in one interaction from the claim.** The reader who wants to verify does not scroll, search, or leave the page. The evidence is one deliberate action from the assertion.

**4. No orphan claims.** Every factual assertion traces to a registered claim in the canonical layer. Claims without a registry entry are not published.

**5. The Six Questions are always visible.** The reader always knows what questions this chapter answers. The questions are stated at the start and answered by the end. No chapter ends without answering all six.

**6. Counterarguments live at the point of disagreement.** Dissenting interpretations are never relegated to a separate section. They appear alongside the claim they challenge, attributed to the historian or school that holds them.

**7. Every chapter has a timeline.** No narrative without chronology. Every chapter must render key events in temporal sequence. Timelines link to the canonical timeline registry.

**8. Learning objectives are stated before narrative begins.** The reader knows what understanding looks like before they start reading. Objectives are specific enough to verify — "By the end of this chapter, you will be able to explain why Nehru chose non-alignment."

**9. The default reading path is the explorer path.** The mode selector is always available but never required. A reader who does nothing sees the most accessible version of the chapter. Depth is opt-in.

**10. Every chapter ends with "What do you want to learn next?"** The final offer is not social sharing or subscription. It is a guided continuation to the next logical knowledge object in the reader's journey.

**11. Reader Agency — the three layers are never blurred.** The reader must always be able to distinguish between documented fact, historical interpretation, and editorial synthesis. The interface must never present interpretation as fact or synthesis as source. Each of the Three-Layer Claim types carries distinct visual and textual treatment. Confidence scores reinforce the distinction. The reader always knows which layer they are reading.
