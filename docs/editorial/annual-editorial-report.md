# Annual Editorial Report

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Operational

The Annual Editorial Report is the institution's self-assessment. It is published on the `/trust` dashboard and forms the core of the Founders' Review. It answers one question for readers and stakeholders: "Did The Breakdown become more trustworthy this year?"

---

## Timing

The Annual Editorial Report is prepared in the first two weeks of January. It covers the previous calendar year. It is published by 31 January.

The report coincides with the annual Founders' Review, where the Editorial Constitution is re-read, the Book of Record is reviewed, and the institution's direction is assessed.

---

## Report structure

### 1. Publication summary

- Chapters published this year (total, by collection)
- Total published chapters to date
- Words published
- Claims registered
- Primary sources cited
- Visual assets verified and published
- Thinker profiles created or updated
- Knowledge Graph nodes created

### 2. Trust Index history

- Institutional Trust Index: start of year, end of year, quarterly snapshots
- Component scores: evidence quality, primary source ratio, review completion, freshness, correction quality, transparency, reader trust, expert review
- Trends: which components improved, which declined, why

### 3. Pipeline performance

- Stories started vs. stories completed
- Average cycle time by stage
- Gate pass rate (first attempt)
- Most common gate failures
- Bottlenecks identified and resolved
- Process improvements implemented

### 4. Correction report

- Total correction submissions received
- Corrections accepted (by category: factual, interpretive, typographical, source-related)
- Corrections rejected (with categories)
- Average time to resolve by priority
- SLA compliance rate
- Corrections that cascaded across multiple chapters
- Systemic error patterns identified and addressed

### 5. Evidence debt

- Total evidence debt at start and end of year
- Claims with insufficient evidence
- Oldest unresolved evidence debt
- Evidence debt reduction initiatives and results

### 6. External engagement

- Academic citations of Breakdown chapters
- University course adoptions (if known)
- Journalist references (if known)
- Policy references (if known)
- Reader trust signals (corrections received — quality and thoughtfulness — not page views)
- Expert review participation (reviewers engaged, reviews completed)

### 7. Institutional changes

- Changes to the Editorial Constitution (if any)
- Changes to the Editorial OS
- Changes to the editorial team
- Changes to the Advisory Board
- New collections started
- Methodology updates

### 8. Challenges and failures

- What went wrong this year
- What was not accomplished
- What the institution learned
- What will change as a result

### 9. Next year's priorities

- Publishing goals
- Quality improvement goals
- Process improvement goals
- Institutional development goals

### 10. Founders' Assessment

The Editor-in-Chief provides a written assessment addressing:

- Is the Editorial Constitution still fit for purpose?
- Are the quality standards being maintained?
- Is the institution becoming more trustworthy?
- What should the institution stop doing?
- What should the institution start doing?

## The Founders' Review

The Annual Editorial Report is presented at the Founders' Review. The Founders' Review is a meeting of:

- Editor-in-Chief
- Editorial Conductor
- Bureau heads (Research, Editorial, Verification, Knowledge Operations)
- Advisory Board members (at least two)

The Founders' Review follows this agenda:

1. **Re-read the Editorial Constitution** — aloud, from preamble to last article
2. **Review the Book of Record** — all decisions, exceptions, and corrections from the year
3. **Analyse the Annual Report** — what the numbers say
4. **Identify standards drift** — where did the institution cut corners?
5. **Set next year's direction** — what the institution will focus on
6. **Update the institution** — deliberate evolution of standards and processes

The Founders' Review is documented in the Book of Record. Its conclusions are published in the Annual Report.

## Publication

The Annual Editorial Report is published in full on the `/trust` dashboard. The only redactions are: personal information (names of individual correction submitters), internal process details that do not affect readers.

The report is linked from the site footer, the `/methodology` page, and every published chapter's metadata.

## Success criteria

The Annual Editorial Report succeeds if:

- A reader can determine whether the institution became more trustworthy
- A stakeholder can assess whether the institution is fulfilling its mission
- The editorial team can identify where to improve
- The Founders' Review produces concrete changes for the next year
- The report is published on time (by 31 January)
