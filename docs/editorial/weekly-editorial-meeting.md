# Weekly Editorial Meeting Agenda

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Operational

The Weekly Editorial Meeting is the standing operational rhythm of the Editorial Office. It runs every week on the same day and time. Duration: 60 minutes.

---

## Standing Agenda

### 1. Chapter status (15 minutes)

Go through every active chapter in the pipeline. For each chapter:

- **Current stage:** Where is it in the pipeline?
- **Status:** Running / Blocked / Waiting for review / Waiting for human approval
- **Days in stage:** How long has it been here?
- **Blockers:** What is preventing advancement?
- **Next action:** What needs to happen before the next meeting?

The Editorial Conductor provides the status board. The meeting does not re-verify — it reviews and acts on blockers.

### 2. Corrections and feedback (10 minutes)

- **New submissions:** Any correction submissions since the last meeting?
- **In progress:** Open correction tickets and their status
- **Patterns:** Are there recurring types of corrections?
- **Reader feedback:** Significant reader feedback received (not individual corrections — patterns and themes)

### 3. This week's priorities (10 minutes)

- **What must be completed this week:** Exactly one thing per person
- **What can slip:** What is important but not urgent
- **Decisions needed:** What requires a group decision before it can proceed

### 4. Process review (10 minutes)

- **Gate failures:** Any stage that failed its quality gate this week? Why?
- **Pipeline metrics:** Cycle time per stage, pass rate per gate
- **One process improvement:** One thing the team can do differently next week

### 5. Next week (10 minutes)

- **Starts:** What new stories should enter the pipeline next week?
- **Reviews:** What upcoming reviews need to be scheduled?
- **Risks:** What might go wrong next week?

### 6. Governance (5 minutes)

- **Constitutional question:** Is anything in the active work raising a constitutional question that needs recording?
- **Precedent:** Is any decision this week setting a precedent that should be recorded in the Book of Record?

---

## Before the meeting

- The Editorial Conductor updates the status board
- The Book of Record is reviewed for any entries since the last meeting
- Correction tickets are compiled
- Each team member prepares their "one thing for this week"

## After the meeting

- Meeting notes are recorded in the Book of Record (decisions only, not full discussion)
- Action items are assigned with owners and deadlines
- The status board is updated with any decisions that change pipeline state
- Decisions that set precedent are flagged for the Monthly Editorial Review

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility in meeting |
|---|---|
| Editor-in-Chief | Chairs the meeting. Makes final decisions on blockers and priorities. |
| Editorial Conductor | Presents status board. Reports pipeline metrics. |
| Research Director | Reports research progress and evidence acquisition status. |
| Narrative Editor(s) | Report writing progress, narrative challenges, blueprint questions. |
| Verification Bureau | Reports verification status and correction triage. |
| Learning Designer | Reports learning section status and reader engagement signals. |
| Knowledge Operations | Reports freshness status, Trust Index, and Book of Record entries. |

## Meeting rules

- No preparation required beyond the status board — the meeting is a review, not a briefing
- Decisions are recorded at the time they are made, not after
- If a decision requires more information, the person responsible brings it to the next meeting
- The meeting starts on time and ends on time
- If there is nothing to discuss in an agenda item, skip it — do not fill the time
