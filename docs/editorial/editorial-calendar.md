# Editorial Calendar

**Verification and review schedule for The Breakdown Knowledge Platform.**

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Draft  
**Last Updated:** July 2026  

---

Unlike a publishing calendar (which schedules release dates), the Editorial Calendar schedules **verification reviews**. Every collection, volume, and chapter receives scheduled review regardless of whether anyone has reported an issue.

The calendar is organised by collection and rotated across the year to ensure all knowledge is reviewed at least annually. Flagship chapters are reviewed every six months.

## Per-Chapter Review Schedule

Flagship chapters: Every 6 months from publication date.

Standard chapters: Every 12 months from publication date.

Stories and investigations: Every 12 months from publication date.

Claims: Continuous — updated whenever new evidence appears.

Sources: Every 12 months — check for continued availability and relevance.

---

## Verification Process

Each review cycle shall:

1. **Check freshness** — Is the knowledge object still current? Has new evidence emerged?
2. **Check sources** — Are all sources still accessible? Have any been retracted or superseded?
3. **Check claims** — Have any claims been challenged? Is the confidence score still appropriate?
4. **Check corrections** — Have any related corrections been issued in other objects?
5. **Update metadata** — Freshness state, last verified date, version number.
6. **Log in Book of Record** — Record what was reviewed and whether changes were needed.

If no changes are needed, the object's last verified date is updated and the freshness state remains Current.

If changes are needed, the object enters the Editorial Workflow pipeline (Article X) at the appropriate stage.

---

## Emergency Reviews

An unscheduled review may be triggered by:

1. Publication of new scholarly work that directly challenges a claim.
2. Declassification of primary sources that change the evidentiary basis.
3. Identification of a factual error (triggers review of all related objects).
4. A credible external challenge from a subject-matter expert.
5. A significant real-world event that changes the relevance or framing of the subject.

Emergency reviews take precedence over scheduled reviews.
