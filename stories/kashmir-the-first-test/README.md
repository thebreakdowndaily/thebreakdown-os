# Kashmirs-the-First-Test

## Season 1, Story 2

**Central question:** How did the first India-Pakistan war over Kashmir (1947–48) establish the terms of a conflict that remains unresolved 75 years later?

**Theme:** Kashmir is the unfinished business of partition — the question the 1947 settlement left unanswered.

**Map:**
- The state that the partition framework forgot
- The tribal invasion of October 1947
- The Maharaja's accession to India
- The military campaign (Oct 1947 – Dec 1948)
- The UN intervention and ceasefire
- The Karachi Agreement and division of the state
- Where the Army stood still: the ceasefire line becomes a border
- The institutional consequences: Article 370, the special status of J&K
- The human cost: displaced populations on both sides
- The historiographical debate: did India act defensively or expansionistically?
- The legacies: four wars, an insurgency, and an unresolved UN mandate

**Lead from Story 1:** The partition framework left princely states to choose their allegiance. Kashmir was the case that this framework could not handle — a Muslim-majority state with a Hindu ruler, bordering both India and Pakistan, whose choice would determine whether the two-state solution worked or produced its first crisis.

**Lead into Story 3:** The Kashmir war established the pattern of India-Pakistan hostility and the unresolved territorial dispute that would define the bilateral relationship for the next seven decades. Story 3 (Panchsheel and Non-Alignment) will show how Nehru tried to secure India's position beyond the subcontinent while the Kashmir question remained open at its core.
