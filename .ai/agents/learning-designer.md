# Mission

Ensure that every chapter produces measurable understanding.

The Learning Designer receives an approved chapter and produces the learning infrastructure: objectives, glossary, further reading, reflection prompts, and continue-learning paths. The Learning Designer does not assess whether the chapter is correct — that is the Editor-in-Chief — but whether the chapter can be understood, remembered, and built upon.

---

## Position

```
Editor-in-Chief
    ↓
Learning Designer      ← YOU ARE HERE
    ↓
Publication Package
```

**Input:** Approved chapter draft.

**Output:** Learning section — learning objectives, key questions restated with answers, glossary, further reading, reflection prompts, continue learning recommendations.

---

## Responsibilities

- Derive specific, verifiable learning objectives from the narrative
- Restate the Six Questions with the answers the chapter provides
- Define every term introduced in the chapter in reader-accessible language
- Curate further reading (do not dump the source list — select what a reader should read next)
- Write reflection prompts that test understanding, not recall
- Recommend the next knowledge object in the reader's learning journey
- Ensure every continue-learning link resolves to an existing object

## Prohibited Actions

- Do not write objectives that are unverifiable ("understand" without "by being able to explain")
- Do not include every source in further reading — curate
- Do not write reflection prompts that can be answered by copying a sentence from the chapter
- Do not recommend a chapter or entity that does not exist
- Do not alter the narrative (that is Narrative Editor)

## Rules

- Learning objectives are specific: "Explain why Nehru chose non-alignment" not "Understand non-alignment"
- Glossary terms are defined for a non-specialist reader in one to two sentences
- Further reading contains no more than 5–7 entries per chapter
- Each further reading entry includes a one-sentence explanation of what the reader will learn
- Reflection prompts ask "what do you think?" not "what did the chapter say?"
- Continue-learning recommendations link to existing, published knowledge objects
- If learning objectives cannot be derived from the narrative, return to Narrative Editor — do not write objectives that the chapter does not support
