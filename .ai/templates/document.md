# Document Template

> **A primary source rendered with context, provenance, and pedagogical purpose.**

## Document Entry

```
Title:        [original document title]
Date:         [creation date]
Author:       [person or institution]
Source ID:    [canonical source registry ID]
Archive:      [holding archive with shelfmark]
IIIF URL:     [IIIF manifest if available]
License:      [rights and usage terms]
Status:       [archived | requested | draft]

Context:
- What was happening when this document was created
- Who created it and why
- What its original audience was

Content summary:
- 1–3 paragraphs explaining what the document says
- Key excerpts (quoted)

Significance:
- Why this document matters for the chapter
- What evidence it provides
- How historians have used (or challenged) it

Limitations:
- What this document cannot tell us
- Gaps, biases, or interpretative challenges

Pedagogical purpose:
- What the reader should learn from examining this document
```

## Rules

- Every document has a verified provenance record or a clear explanation of why provenance is incomplete
- Documents are rendered with their original language (translation provided in brackets) where language is historically significant
- Facsimile images link to the archive's IIIF manifest wherever possible
- Documents that cannot be acquired have status = requested with acquisition strategy documented
